Nolan Bushnell
A founder of Atari and Chuck E. Cheese's

Here's a timeline of Bushnell's life:

  • 1943: Born in Clearfield, Utah
  • 1969: Formed Syzygy with Ted Dabney
  • 1972: Atari, Inc. founded
  • 1975: Pong released
  • 1976: Bushnell refuses to invest in Apple
  • 1977: Atari VCS (2600) launched
  • 1977: Bushnell purchases Pizza Time Theatre from Warner Communications
  • 1978: Bushnell forced out of Atari
  • 1982: Atari has $1.3 billion in annual sales
  • 1983: Sells off subsidiaries Sente (to Bally) and Kadabrascope (to Lucas)
  • 1983: Joe Keenan, president of Chuck E. Cheese, resigns amid financial issues
  • 1984: Atari crashes and is split and sold off
  • 1984: Chuck E. Cheese's Pizza Time Theatres declares bankruptcy
  • 1984: Etak founded. Early backbone for digital mapping and navigation systems
  • 1995: Bushnell becomes senior consultant for Aristo Intl.
  • 2000: Etak becomes wholly owned subsidiary of Tele Atlas
  • 2005: uWink Media Bistro announced
  • 2006: First uWink Bistro opens in Woodland Hills. Second opened in Hollywood
  • 2008: Third uWink opened in Southern California and one in Mountain View
  • 2010: Bushnell and Time Virden join Atari, SA Board of Directors
  • 2013: BrainRush, a company Bushnell founded and is CEO of currently, rolls out language learning software platform
  • 2016: Bushnell joins board of directors of MGT Capital Investments

Nolan is one of the brightest minds in cyber technology. In his career, he has founded more than 20 high tech companies, giving him unprecedented knowledge of the tech industry. As a director, he will help MGT identify and cultivate the necessary strategic partnerships to position the company as the world leader in cyber security. -- John McAfee
